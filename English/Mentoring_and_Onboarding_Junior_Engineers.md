# Mentoring and onboarding junior engineers

## Purpose
Help new teammates become **productive and confident** without burning mentor time. Good onboarding is **structured**, **measurable**, and **kind**.

## First two weeks (template)
- **Day 0–1**: environment setup checklist, repo map, how to ask for help, team rituals.
- **Day 2–5**: one **small merged PR** (docs, test, logging fix) to learn CI and review culture.
- **Week 2**: first **bounded feature** with a written **design sketch** reviewed by a buddy.

## Mentoring behaviors that work
- **Explain the why**: constraints, users, failure modes—not only steps.
- **Pair on the fuzzy parts**, let them solo the repetitive parts.
- **Normalize questions**; answer in public channels when it helps the group.
- **Debrief reviews**: translate feedback into one or two habits to practice next.

## Anti-patterns
- “Read the codebase” without a **guided tour** or milestones.
- Taking the keyboard for days; they learn muscle memory by typing.
- Vague praise (“great job”) without **what** was strong; same for corrections.

## Measuring success
- They can **run, test, and deploy** the main service alone.
- They can **trace a request** end-to-end at a basic level.
- They participate in **incident or bug triage** with support.
