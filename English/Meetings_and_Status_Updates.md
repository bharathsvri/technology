# Meetings, Stand-ups, and Status Updates

## Purpose
Meetings exist to **synchronize**, **decide**, or **unblock**. Everything else is better async (doc, ticket, chat with a link).

## Stand-up (Typical Format)
In **under two minutes**, cover:
1. **Yesterday**: what shipped toward the sprint goal.
2. **Today**: the next concrete deliverable.
3. **Blockers**: who/what you need, with a **clear ask** and **deadline** if urgent.

### Strong Example
“Yesterday I merged the payment validation service and verified it in QA. Today I’ll finish the idempotency tests and open the PR for review. I’m blocked on the sandbox merchant ID—@Priya, can you share by noon?”

### Weak Example
“Yesterday I worked on tickets. Today more tickets. No blockers.” → lacks traceability to outcomes.

## Status Email / Chat Update
Use **SCQA** (Situation, Complication, Question, Answer) for leadership:
- **Situation**: where we are on the plan.
- **Complication**: risk or variance (delay, defect rate, dependency slip).
- **Question**: the decision or help needed.
- **Answer**: your recommendation with options.

Keep it **short**; link to specs, dashboards, and tickets.

## Decision Meetings
- Circulate a **one-page pre-read** with options and criteria.
- Capture **decisions** in writing: owner, date, scope, explicit non-goals.
- Assign **action items** with one owner each.

## Incident / War-Room Communication
- **Single incident commander** voice externally if required.
- Internal updates: **timestamped facts**, impact, mitigation, ETA for next update.
- Avoid blame during the bridge; schedule a **blameless postmortem**.

## Etiquette
- **On time**; decline invites without agendas when policy allows.
- **Agenda in invite**; take notes or rotate a scribe role.
- **Summary message** after meetings with decisions and links.

## Cultural Note
Match client formality: some teams prefer **first names** and Slack updates; others expect **structured weekly reports**. Mirror their channel and tone without losing precision.
