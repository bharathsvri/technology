# Technical Interviews: STAR Stories for Developers

Interviewers often ask **behavioral** questions (“Tell me about a conflict…”, “A time you missed a deadline…”). The **STAR** method keeps answers structured and credible.

## STAR Outline
- **S — Situation**: one sentence of context (team, product, constraint).
- **T — Task**: your responsibility, not the whole company’s.
- **A — Action**: what **you** did, with tools and trade-offs.
- **R — Result**: measurable outcome, learning, or follow-up process change.

## Example Skeleton (Technical Debt)
- **S**: Legacy payment service with flaky integration tests blocking releases.
- **T**: I owned stabilizing CI for the payment squad.
- **A**: Added Testcontainers-based DB fixtures, shrank flaky sleeps, parallelized safe suites, paired with QA on smoke checklist.
- **R**: CI pass rate from ~70% to 98% over two sprints; release cadence moved weekly → twice weekly.

## Good Practices
- Prepare **5–7** stories spanning conflict, leadership without authority, failure/learning, customer urgency, and cross-team alignment.
- Quantify when possible (**latency**, **incident MTTR**, **defect rate**).
- Avoid blaming individuals; describe **systems** and **behaviors**.

## Common Prompts to Prepare
- Time you **disagreed** with a design decision.
- **Production incident** you mitigated or postmortemed.
- **Mentoring** or onboarding a junior developer.
- Working with a **difficult stakeholder** or shifting requirements.
