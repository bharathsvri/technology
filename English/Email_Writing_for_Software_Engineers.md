# Email Writing for Software Engineers

Email is still the **default** record for decisions, approvals, and cross-team alignment. Clear email reduces **meetings** and **rework**.

---

## Structure that works

1. **Subject**: outcome-oriented — `Action: approve DB migration for billing (May 12)` not `Question`.
2. **First line**: **context + ask** in two sentences (busy readers stop after the preview).
3. **Body**: bullets for **options**, **risks**, **deadline**, **who** decides.
4. **Close**: explicit **“Please reply by …”** or **“No objections by … means go-ahead.”**

---

## Tone

- **Neutral and specific**; avoid blame (“you broke”) → use **timeline + impact**.
- **Assume good intent**; escalate tone only through **manager/channel norms**.

---

## Technical content

- Paste **error signatures** (first/last lines), **request ids**, **timestamps UTC**, not whole logs.
- **Link** to tickets, docs, and **dashboards** instead of duplicating volatile data.

---

## When not to email

- **Incident active** → use war-room chat + status page; email **postmortem** summary.
- **Quick clarifications** → chat; **contract changes** → email or ticket.
