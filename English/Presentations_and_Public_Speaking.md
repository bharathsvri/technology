# Presentations and Public Speaking (Technical Context)

## Outcomes
Deliver **clear**, **credible**, and **calm** presentations to clients and internal stakeholders—design reviews, sprint demos, incident postmortems, and architecture walkthroughs.

## Structure (20-Minute Technical Talk)
1. **Context (1–2 min)**: problem, audience, constraints (SLA, budget, compliance).
2. **Goal**: what decision or alignment you need by the end.
3. **Approach**: options considered, trade-offs, recommendation.
4. **Evidence**: diagrams, benchmarks, risks, rollout plan.
5. **Q&A setup**: explicit “parking lot” for deep dives.

## Slides
- **One idea per slide**; large fonts; high contrast.
- Prefer **diagrams** over paragraphs; show **interfaces** and **data flow**, not walls of code.
- Animate only when it clarifies sequence; avoid decorative motion.

## Delivery
- **Slow down** on numbers and acronyms; define terms once.
- **Eye contact** / camera discipline in remote calls; hide self-view if it distracts.
- **Record and review** yourself for filler words (“um”, “like”).

## Handling Questions
- **Clarify** ambiguous questions before answering.
- **Admit unknowns**: “I don’t have that number now; I’ll follow up by Thursday with X owner cc’d.”
- **Bridge** off-topic questions: “Great point—let’s take it offline so we finish the decision on scope.”

## Demos
- **Rehearse** on clean data; have a **fallback video** if live demos are fragile.
- Narrate **intent** while clicking: “I’m showing how an error surfaces to the support user.”

## Remote-Specific
- **Headset** mic; mute when not speaking in large calls.
- **Share only the window** needed; pause notifications.

## Practice Plan
- Three **full dry runs** for high-stakes talks.
- Timebox sections; cut depth before cutting clarity.
